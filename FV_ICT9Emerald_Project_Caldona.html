Always keep a fire extinguisher

They can help put out small fires in case of emergency and prevent any further damage caused by fire. To use a fire extinguisher, follow the PASS procedure. Pull the pin, Aim at the fire, Squeeze the lever, and Sweep side to side.

Don't leave the stove unattended

Make sure to always keep an eye on the stove while you're cooking. It only takes seconds for your stove to overheat and start a fire. If you have to leave the stove for a short while, don't forget to turn it off.

Keep candles, oil and gas lamps away from objects that could start fire

Make sure that candles or lamps are placed far from things like curtains or paper because fire could start spreading quickly and cause huge damage. Make sure to never leave candles unattended especially around children or pets.

Don't overload electrical circuits

Overloading circuits are known to be one of the major causes of residential fires. Overloading leads to overheating and would eventually start a fire. Don't exceed the rated load of a circuit and unplug sockets that you dont'use in order to prevent this.
